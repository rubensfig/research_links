    Author Klaus Wehrle
    Abstract Programmable network devices are on the rise with many applications ranging from improved network management to accelerating and offloading parts of distributed systems. Processor-based SmartNICs, match-action-based switches, and FPGA devices offer on-path programmability. Whereas processor-based SmartNICs are much easier and more versatile to program, they have the huge disadvantage that the resulting throughput may vary strongly and is not easily predictable even to the programmer.
